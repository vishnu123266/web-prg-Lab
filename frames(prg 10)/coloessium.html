The Colosseum, Rome

The Colosseum is a massive elliptical amphitheater in Rome, Italy, built by the Flavian emperors between 70-80 AD to host public spectacles like gladiatorial contests and animal hunts. Also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, it was a significant symbol of Roman power and ingenuity, designed to hold tens of thousands of spectators. Despite centuries of damage from earthquakes and neglect, it remains the largest ancient amphitheater ever built and a major tourist attraction.
